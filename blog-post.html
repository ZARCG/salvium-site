What are Zero-Knowledge Proofs?

A zero-knowledge proof is a method by which one party (the prover) can prove to another party (the verifier) that a statement is true without conveying any additional information apart from the fact that the statement is indeed true.

"The ability to prove something without revealing the underlying data is revolutionary for privacy-preserving technologies." - Salvium Whitepaper

Technical Implementation

Here's a simple example of how ZK-proofs work in practice:

// Example ZK-Proof Implementation
protocol.verify(transaction, {
    proof: zkProof,
    publicInputs: publicData,
    privateInputs: privateData
});
